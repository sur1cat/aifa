# User Personas

## Persona 1: Mindful Maria

### Demographics
- **Age**: 32
- **Occupation**: UX Designer at a tech company
- **Location**: San Francisco, CA
- **Income**: $120,000/year
- **Device**: iPhone 15 Pro, MacBook Pro

### Background
Maria is a design-conscious professional who values intentional living. She's tried multiple habit tracking apps but always returns to pen and paper because digital solutions feel "too heavy." She reads productivity blogs, practices morning meditation, and is always looking for ways to simplify her life.

### Goals
1. Build consistent morning and evening routines
2. Track fitness habits (yoga 3x/week, 10k steps daily)
3. Reduce screen time and social media usage
4. Feel in control without feeling overwhelmed

### Frustrations
- Apps with too many features and settings
- Gamification that feels childish
- Having to maintain multiple apps
- Notifications that interrupt focus

### Behaviors
- Checks habits in the morning and before bed
- Prefers visual/aesthetic interfaces
- Willing to pay for premium apps that respect her time
- Shares progress with accountability partner

### Quote
> "I just want to check off my habits and move on with my day. Why does every app try to be my life coach?"

### Jobs To Be Done
- **When** I wake up, **I want to** see what habits I need to do today, **so I can** start my morning with intention
- **When** I complete a habit, **I want to** feel a small moment of satisfaction, **so I can** stay motivated without big distractions
- **When** I'm reviewing my week, **I want to** see my consistency at a glance, **so I can** understand my patterns

## Persona 2: Busy Boris

### Demographics
- **Age**: 28
- **Occupation**: Startup Founder
- **Location**: Austin, TX
- **Income**: Variable (raised seed round)
- **Device**: iPhone 14, various laptops

### Background
Boris is building his first startup and struggles with work-life balance. He works 60+ hours a week and knows he needs to take better care of himself. He's tried complex systems (Notion, Todoist + Streaks + YNAB) but can't maintain them because his schedule is chaotic.

### Goals
1. Build basic health habits (sleep, water, exercise)
2. Track simple financial goals (savings rate)
3. Have one app instead of many
4. Spend minimum time on tracking

### Frustrations
- Context switching between apps
- Complex setup and configuration
- Forgetting to log things
- Feeling guilty about broken streaks

### Behaviors
- Uses apps in bursts (heavy for a week, then abandons)
- Prefers defaults over customization
- Often catches up on tracking at end of day
- Makes purchasing decisions quickly if value is clear

### Quote
> "I don't have time to maintain a productivity system. I need something that takes 30 seconds and just works."

### Jobs To Be Done
- **When** I have a spare moment, **I want to** quickly log today's habits, **so I can** maintain momentum without deep focus
- **When** I miss a day, **I want to** easily catch up without judgment, **so I can** stay engaged with the system
- **When** I'm considering my finances, **I want to** see basic trends in one place, **so I can** make decisions without spreadsheets

---

## Persona Comparison

| Attribute | Mindful Maria | Busy Boris |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Primary need** | Simplicity & beauty | Speed & convenience |
| **Time for tracking** | 5-10 min/day | < 1 min/day |
| **Customization** | Moderate | Minimal |
| **Price sensitivity** | Low | Low |
| **Streak importance** | High | Low |
| **Device usage** | Morning & evening ritual | Throughout day, quick bursts |
| **Biggest fear** | Another app to maintain | Wasting time on meta-work |

## Design Implications

1. **For Maria**: Beautiful, calm aesthetic. Satisfying completion animations. Weekly reflection features.

2. **For Boris**: Fast interactions (< 3 seconds). Smart defaults. Batch catch-up mode. No guilt for missed days.

3. **For Both**:
   - Minimal onboarding
   - One unified app
   - Offline-first
   - No notification spam
